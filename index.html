About

I was bit by the programming bug in my Junior year of high school when I was enrolled in a program at the local technical school. Ever since I've been studying and working hard to prepare myself for my career as a programmer. I didn't quit when my school closed when I was near completion of my Associate's Degree. In fact, this resulted in me studying way more courses than the average Associate's graduate.


I started with an interest in software development that leveraged OOP. As I finished my degree I began to enjoy web development more and more. I would say I am at a crossroads where I could be pulled down either direction: web dev or software dev. Either way, the skills I've learned have turned me into a well-rounded programmer.
